1. The "Changing Cities" (Market) Bug
You mentioned having issues when changing cities. Looking at your leads.tsx file, I found exactly why this is happening.

When a user is on Page 3 of the "Dallas" market and switches the city to "Austin" via your useMarket() dropdown, the app queries Page 3 of Austin. If Austin only has 1 page of leads, the UI will show a blank screen ("No leads found").

The Fix: You need to add activeMarket?.id to your pagination reset useEffect so that changing markets resets the view to Page 1.

In client/src/pages/leads.tsx:

TypeScript
// Add activeMarket?.id to the dependency array
useEffect(() => {
  setPage(1);
}, [
  activeMarket?.id, // <-- ADD THIS
  debouncedSearch, county, minScore, zoning, status, hasPhone, 
  minRoofAge, minRoofArea, lastHailWithin, claimWindowOpen, 
  minPropertyValue, ownershipStructure, roofType, riskTier, sortBy
]);
2. Making Generative AI "Run" and Feel Alive
Your backend has an incredible AI foundation (ai-audit-results, grok-proxy, data-audit-agent). However, right now, it feels like AI is just a background batch job. To make the system feel "intuitive" and AI-driven, you need to bring the AI into the user interface.

Recommendation: Implement "Natural Language Filtering"
Instead of forcing roofers to click 14 different dropdowns (Zoning, Roof Age, Hail Exposure, etc.), give them an AI search bar at the top of the Leads page.

User types: "Show me leads in Tarrant county with a roof older than 15 years that had hail recently."

Send this to your /api/ops/grok-ask endpoint.

Have the LLM return a JSON object mapping to your exact LeadFilter schema:

JSON
{ "county": "Tarrant", "minRoofAge": 15, "lastHailWithin": 12 }
Feed that JSON directly into your applyFilterPreset() function.

This changes your app from a "database viewer" into an "AI Assistant that does the hunting for you."
